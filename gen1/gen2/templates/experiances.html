Emily Turner

This was one of my darkest times.

Breaking Free: A Journey from
Perfectionism and Addiction to Self-Love and Sobriety

Beneath the surface, however, I always felt a lot of pressure to be perfect. In my early years, the pressure to be perfect consumed me. As the eldest of 12 grandchildren, the weight of expectations fueled anxiety since the age of 5. At 15, my world shattered when my parents decided to divorce, forcing us to coexist until the legalities were settled. Switching to a public school in junior year left me feeling lost and out of control, leading to the start of an eating disorder as a way to regain control.
I resisted alcohol and drugs, but a turning point came on New Year's Eve. Despite hating the taste, alcohol became an escape. The downward spiral continued with declining grades and trouble at home. Desperate to finish a paper, I tried Adderall, unknowingly setting off a dangerous cycle of dependence. Eventually, I convinced a doctor I had ADHD, leading to a prescription that only worsened my situation.

My life spiraled out of control. I lost friends, alienated family, and sank into despair. At 17, I attempted suicide. Surviving, I entered a dual diagnosis treatment center, finding support in AA and dedicated individuals. Working through the 12 steps, prayer, and meditation, I gradually overcame addiction, anxiety, and depression.

Rebuilding my life, I completed high school and earned a degree in Elementary Education. Now, six years into teaching first grade, my passion is helping teenagers battling addiction, emphasizing self-love and acceptance. I've learned to be kind to myself, understanding the importance of personal growth. Every day, I strive to be a better version of myself.
